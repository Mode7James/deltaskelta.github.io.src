- vectors are an `[n x 1]` matrix
- matrix addition only works on matrices of the same dimension
- matrix multiplication/division by a scalar multiplies/divides each element by the scalar
- a 4x3 matrix multiplied by a 4x1 vector works like this

<BlockMath math="
  \begin{bmatrix}
   1 & 0 & 3 & 2 \\
   5 & 3 & 6 & 8 \\
   4 & 9 & 1 & 3
  \end{bmatrix} *
  \begin{bmatrix}
   3 \\
   2 \\
   1 \\
   6
  \end{bmatrix} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
   1*3 + 0*2 + 3*1 + 2*6 \\
   5*3 + 3*2 + 6*1 + 8*6 \\
   4*3 + 9*2 + 1*1 + 3*6
  \end{bmatrix} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  18 \\
  75 \\
  49
  \end{bmatrix}
" />

- matrix multiplication is not commutative but it is associative. Meaning that the operations can happen in any order
but the positions cannot be switched.
- if one of the matrices being multiplied is an identity matrix, then multiplication is commutative.

<BlockMath math="
  \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 0 & 0 \\
    0 & 1 & 0 \\
    0 & 0 & 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  \quad = Identity Matrix = I_{nxn}
" />

- `m by m` matrices haev an inverse that when multiplied by itself, gives the identity matrix.

<BlockMath math="3(3^-1) = 1" />
<BlockMath math="A(A^-1) = A^-1A = I" />

- the number of columns in the first matrix must be equal to the number of rows in the second matrix

<BlockMath math="
  A =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    1 & 2 & 3 \\
    7 & 3 & 1 \\
    5 & 9 & 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  B =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    0 & 1 \\
    7 & 1
  \end{bmatrix}
  C =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    4 & 2 \\
    2 & 8 \\
    3 & 5
  \end{bmatrix}
" />

In the above example matrices, <InlineMath math="A * C" /> is ok <InlineMath math="A * B" /> is not ok,
<InlineMath math="B * C" /> is not ok, but <InlineMath math="C * B" /> is ok
